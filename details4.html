Korean War

The Korean War was a conflict that occurred between June 25, 1950, and July 27, 1953, primarily between North Korea, supported by China and the Soviet Union, and South Korea, supported by the United Nations forces led by the United States.

The war began when North Korean forces invaded South Korea, leading to a swift response from the United Nations. The conflict saw significant battles, including the Battle of Inchon and the Battle of Chosin Reservoir.

The Korean War ended with an armistice agreement signed on July 27, 1953, but technically, the two Koreas remain in a state of war, as a formal peace treaty was never signed.
